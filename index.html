Research Projects

Localized surface plasmon resonance driven laser lithography on highly insulating substrates (Supervisors- Vasyl Shvalya, Damjan Vengust, Uros Cvelbar- Institute Jozef Stefan, Slovenia) (Master thesis)

  • Use localized surface plasmon resonance (LSPR) to engineer highly localized melting of silver nanoparticles on nanostructured substrates using low power continuous wave laser
  • Building own theoretical models from scratch using COMSOL to explain heat dissipation, temperature spike and melting at resonant frequencies on the different substrates used
  • Building surrogate machine learning models to predict and interpolate nanoparticle nucleation and morphology based on experimental parameters
  • Developing customized SEM analysis code/programme in MATLAB for nanoparticle morphology analysis on flat and nanostructured substrates
  • Manuscript in progress

Designing, fabricating and enhancing triboelectric nanogenerators with chemically altered, electrospun biopolymers for wearable electronics: collaboration between Jozef Stefan Institute and the European Space Agency (Supervisor: Marija Vukomanović- Institute Jozef Stefan, Slovenia):

  • Engineered enhancement of triboelectric properties of electrospun PLA nanofibers by adding ionic fillers for interfacial maxwell wagner sillars polarization, piezoelectric beta phase promoting fillers etc..
  • Carefully prepared the precursor solution by optimizing polymer weight and ionic fillers for a stable electrospinning jet; the rheological features (viscosity) of the precursor were found to greatly influence the surface morphology, fiber diameter, contact surface (analyzed in SEM) and hence triboelectric performance
  • Achieved >16X increase in triboelectric performance by optimizing ionic filler weight percent compared to polymer
  • Collaboration with the European Space Agency, Tekstina and Jozef Stefan Institute. Supervisor: Dr Marija Vukomanović
  • Manuscript in progress

Atomistic modelling (at supercomputer LEONARDO) of intercalated borophene and boron nanostructures in graphite (HOPG) for heterostructure synthesis, supported by experiments (Supervisors: Manuela Scarselli, Conor Hogan- University of Rome Tor Vergata, National Research Council, Italy):

  • Carried out a full scale phase transition study and calculated STM study of boron nanostructures in graphitic confinement by gradual addition of atoms to:
    • Explain the observed experimental STM features
    • Prove nucleation of 2D boron sheet (borophene) in graphitic confinement at room temperature, while conventional borophene synthesis routes involve silver (111)/metallic templates or substrates, and ~400 K temperatures
  • Achieved a 98% match with experimental features, and observed a possible favorable nucleation of borophene in graphitic confinement conditions
  • DFT simulation of intercalated borophene/boron cluster under graphite for a novel synthesis of heterostructures for gas sensing AND battery applications (May 2025- Present), University of Rome Tor Vergata, Italy
  • Manuscript in progress

Synthesis of lignocellulosic bioplastic film from artichoke waste (Supervisors: Prof Dr Emanuela Gatto, Dr Raffaella Lettieri- University of Rome Tor Vergata, Italy)

Worked with a group of Master's level researchers, using artichoke leaves to extract lignocellulose via alkaline hydrolysis, making a bioplastic film out of the extracted lignucellulose, and characterizing its mechanical properties (deriving secondary properties via calculations), glass transition temperature via differential scanning calorimetry (DSC), chemical composition via FTIR (and determining crystallinity by calculations), hydrophobicity and water vapor permeability (WVP).

Sustainability analysis of permanent magnets for the energy transition (Supervisors: Alexander Nomine, Stephane Mangin- University of Lorraine, France)

Calculating overall sustainability of major permanent magnets, broadly divided into RE (rare earth) based PM’s (NdFeB, SmCo) and non-RE PM’s (strontium hexaferrites, MnAlC) in terms of environmental footprint, performance, supply chain stability and price.
